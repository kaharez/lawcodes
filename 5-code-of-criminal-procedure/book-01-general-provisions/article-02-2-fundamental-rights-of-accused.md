# Article 2 â€“ Fundamental Rights of Accused

1. Every person accused of an offence has the right to:
   (a) be informed promptly of the charge;
   (b) be presumed innocent until proven guilty;
   (c) remain silent and not be compelled to testify;
   (d) legal representation;
   (e) a fair and public hearing;
   (f) examine witnesses;
   (g) interpretation if needed;
   (h) reasonable time to prepare a defense.

2. These rights may not be derogated except as expressly permitted by law.
