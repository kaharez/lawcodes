# Article 4 â€“ Appointment and Qualifications of Curator, Tutor, or Advisor

1. The curator, tutor, or judicial advisor shall be appointed by the court or competent authority, taking into account:
    a. the wishes and preferences of the protected person, expressed currently or in the past;
    b. the suitability, willingness, and availability of proposed candidates;
    c. the relationship between the candidate and the protected person;
    d. the candidate's ability to fulfill the duties of the position;
    e. any conflicts of interest.

2. Priority shall be given, where appropriate and in the person's best interests, to:
    a. the spouse or partner of the protected person;
    b. close family members, including adult children, parents, or siblings;
    c. close friends or persons who have maintained a significant relationship with the protected person.

3. Where no suitable family member or friend is available or willing, the court may appoint:
    a. a professional curator, tutor, or advisor;
    b. a public curator or official charged with the protection of incapable persons;
    c. a non-profit organisation specialising in the protection of vulnerable adults.

4. No person may be appointed as curator, tutor, or advisor if they:
    a. are a minor or themselves under a regime of protection;
    b. have been convicted of an offence involving dishonesty, fraud, abuse, or neglect of vulnerable persons;
    c. are insolvent or have demonstrated serious mismanagement of their own affairs;
    d. have a conflict of interest that would impair their ability to act in the protected person's best interests.

5. Upon appointment, the curator, tutor, or advisor must take an oath or make a solemn affirmation to faithfully discharge their duties in the best interests of the protected person.

6. A curator to property must, before assuming office:
    a. prepare an inventory of the protected person's property;
    b. post security or a bond, if required by the court, to guarantee faithful performance of duties;
    c. establish separate accounts for the protected person's property and funds.

7. The curator, tutor, or advisor is entitled to reasonable compensation for services rendered and reimbursement of expenses, to be paid from the protected person's property if sufficient, or otherwise as determined by the court or provided by law.
