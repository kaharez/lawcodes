# Article 5 â€“ Names and Identity

1. Every national of the state shall bear one or more given names and a family name, as recorded in the civil registers.
2. Noble titles and heraldic designations may be used only by those lawfully entitled to them and duly registered with the Royal Chancellery.
3. No person may assume or use, without lawful authority, a name, title, or heraldic designation that does not belong to them.
4. The right to use a name recorded in the civil registers shall be protected under the laws of the state and may not be arbitrarily denied or altered, except by decree or judicial decision.
