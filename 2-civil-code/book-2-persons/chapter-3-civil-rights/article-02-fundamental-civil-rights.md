# Article 2 â€“ Fundamental Civil Rights

1. The dignity of every person is recognised and protected under the laws of the state.
2. All nationals enjoy the right to personal identity, including the protection of their name, family relations, and reputation.
3. Family life is protected; no person shall be arbitrarily deprived of their family relations as recorded in the civil registers.
4. Privacy in personal and family matters is respected, except as may be lawfully required for the maintenance of the civil registers, the protection of public order, or the fulfilment of sovereign duties.
5. No one shall be compelled to disclose personal information beyond what is required by law or decree.
