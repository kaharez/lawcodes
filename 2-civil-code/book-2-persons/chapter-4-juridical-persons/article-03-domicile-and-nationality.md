# Article 3 â€“ Domicile and Nationality

1. The domicile of a juridical person is at the place designated as its registered office in its constitutive documents or registration.

2. A juridical person may change its domicile by amending its constitutive documents in accordance with the procedures prescribed by law and by notifying the appropriate registry or authority.

3. A juridical person possesses Kaharagian nationality if:
    a. it is incorporated or established under the laws of Kaharagia;
    b. its registered office is located in Kaharagia or, in the absence of territory, its effective management and control are exercised by Kaharagian nationals or by the Sovereign;
    c. it is granted Kaharagian nationality by sovereign decree in recognition of its connection to Kaharagia or its service to the Kaharagian community.

4. A juridical person of foreign nationality may be recognized and permitted to operate or hold property in Kaharagia or among Kaharagian nationals, subject to such conditions and restrictions as are imposed by law or decree.

5. The law governing the internal affairs, governance, and capacity of a juridical person is the law of the state under which it is incorporated or established, unless the law of Kaharagia provides otherwise for juridical persons possessing Kaharagian nationality.
