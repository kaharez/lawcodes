# Article 3 â€“ Complicity and Participation

1. A person is criminally liable as principal if they commit the offence alone or jointly with another.

2. A person is criminally liable as accomplice if they intentionally assist, enable, encourage, or facilitate the commission of an offence.

3. A person is criminally liable as instigator if they intentionally induce another to commit an offence.

4. Accomplices and instigators are punishable with the penalty provided for the principal offender unless the law specifies a lesser penalty.

5. Withdrawal of participation before the offence is carried out may be considered in mitigation.
