# Article 1 â€“ Conditions for Trial in Absence

1. A person may be tried in their absence where:
    a. they have been duly notified of the proceedings by reasonable and accessible means;
    b. they fail to appear without lawful excuse; or
    c. their whereabouts are unknown despite reasonable efforts to locate them.

2. Notification may be made through:
    a. electronic communication to registered addresses;
    b. publication on the designated State portal; or
    c. any other method prescribed by procedural law.

3. The court shall ensure that the accused is represented by counsel, appointed where necessary to safeguard the integrity of the proceedings.
