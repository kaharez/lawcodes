# Article 2 â€“ Rights of the Accused

1. The accused tried in absence shall retain all rights afforded under Kaharagian law, including:
    (a) the right to counsel;
    (b) the right to have evidence examined and challenged on their behalf;
    (c) the right to be informed of the charges, evidence, and judgment;
    (d) the right to appeal or seek rehearing upon appearance.

2. Defence counsel shall act in the best interests of the accused and may present evidence, call witnesses, and make submissions as though the accused were present.
