# Article 1 â€“ Endangering Public Health

1. It is an offence to engage in conduct that knowingly or recklessly creates a serious risk to public health or safety.

2. Endangering public health includes:
    a. adulterating, contaminating, or poisoning food, water, medicines, or other products intended for human consumption;
    b. selling, distributing, or supplying unsafe, contaminated, or adulterated products;
    c. knowingly spreading infectious diseases or failing to comply with quarantine or isolation orders;
    d. operating food production, processing, or service facilities in grossly unsanitary or unsafe conditions;
    e. manufacturing, selling, or distributing counterfeit or substandard medicines or medical devices;
    f. engaging in practices that create serious public health hazards; or
    g. intentionally or recklessly causing pollution or contamination of air, water, or soil in circumstances that endanger public health.

3. Endangering public health is punished under Penalty Band D.

4. The penalty is aggravated to Penalty Band E where:
    a. the conduct results in death, serious illness, or injury;
    b. the conduct affects a large number of persons or creates widespread risk;
    c. the conduct involves intentional poisoning or contamination;
    d. the conduct is motivated by profit or commercial gain;
    e. the offender holds a professional or official position of responsibility for public health or safety;
    f. the conduct involves particularly dangerous substances or pathogens; or
    g. the offender has previous convictions for public health offences.

5. Violation of quarantine, isolation, or public health emergency orders is punished under Penalty Band B, or Penalty Band C where the violation results in transmission of disease or endangers others.

6. The court may order remediation, confiscation of unsafe products, and prohibition from engaging in food, pharmaceutical, or health-related activities.
