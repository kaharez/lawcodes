# Article 3 â€“ Illegal Waste Disposal and Hazardous Materials

1. It is an offence to dispose of, dump, export, import, transport, or store waste, hazardous materials, or toxic substances in violation of environmental laws or in a manner that creates risk to human health or the environment.

2. Illegal waste disposal and hazardous materials offences include:
    a. illegal dumping of waste, including household, commercial, or industrial waste;
    b. disposing of hazardous waste, toxic substances, or dangerous materials without proper authorization or in violation of safety standards;
    c. exporting or importing hazardous waste or toxic substances in violation of international agreements;
    d. abandoning hazardous materials or failing to properly dispose of dangerous substances;
    e. operating illegal waste disposal sites or facilities;
    f. concealing or misrepresenting the nature of waste or hazardous materials;
    g. disposing of electronic waste, batteries, or other special waste improperly;
    h. marine dumping or ocean disposal in violation of maritime environmental laws; or
    i. any other unlawful disposal or handling of waste creating environmental or health risks.

3. Illegal waste disposal and hazardous materials offences are punished under Penalty Band C.

4. The penalty is aggravated to Penalty Band D where:
    a. the waste or materials are highly toxic, radioactive, or pose severe health or environmental risks;
    b. the illegal disposal causes significant pollution or environmental damage;
    c. the illegal disposal affects water sources, agricultural land, or populated areas;
    d. the illegal disposal is systematic, commercial, or involves large quantities;
    e. the offence involves transboundary movement of hazardous waste;
    f. the offence involves deliberate concealment or false documentation;
    g. the offender holds a professional or official position of responsibility for waste management or environmental protection; or
    h. the offender has previous convictions for environmental offences.

5. Export of hazardous waste to jurisdictions without adequate facilities for safe disposal, in violation of the Basel Convention or other international agreements, is punished under Penalty Band D.

6. Juridical persons may be held liable for illegal waste disposal and hazardous materials offences committed in the course of their operations.

7. The court may order cleanup and remediation at the offender's expense, confiscation of equipment and vehicles, prohibition from waste management activities, and payment of environmental damages.

8. Persons who discover illegal dumping or hazardous materials and report them to authorities shall not be prosecuted for possession or handling if the discovery was inadvertent and the person cooperated fully with authorities.

9. This Article gives effect to Kaharagia's obligations under the Basel Convention on the Control of Transboundary Movements of Hazardous Wastes and other international environmental agreements.
