# Article 1 â€“ Environmental Pollution and Damage

1. It is an offence to intentionally or recklessly cause significant pollution, contamination, or damage to the environment in violation of environmental laws or regulations.

2. Environmental pollution and damage includes:
    (a) discharging, dumping, or releasing pollutants, toxic substances, or hazardous waste into air, water, or soil;
    (b) operating industrial, commercial, or other activities without required environmental permits or in violation of environmental standards;
    (c) illegally disposing of hazardous waste or dangerous materials;
    (d) causing significant destruction or degradation of ecosystems, habitats, or protected areas;
    (e) illegal deforestation, land clearance, or destruction of vegetation;
    (f) illegal mining, extraction, or exploitation of natural resources;
    (g) killing, harming, or trafficking in protected or endangered species;
    (h) illegal fishing, hunting, or harvesting of wildlife in violation of conservation laws; or
    (i) any other conduct causing significant environmental harm.

3. Environmental pollution and damage is punished under Penalty Band D.

4. The penalty is aggravated to Penalty Band E where:
    (a) the pollution or damage is severe, widespread, or irreversible;
    (b) the pollution or damage affects protected areas, endangered species, or ecosystems of particular importance;
    (c) the pollution or damage endangers human health or safety;
    (d) the offence is committed for commercial profit or as part of organized criminal activity;
    (e) the offence involves large-scale illegal trafficking in wildlife, timber, or natural resources;
    (f) the offender holds a position of responsibility for environmental protection or regulation;
    (g) the offender has previous convictions for environmental crimes; or
    (h) the offence involves transnational environmental crime.

5. Juridical persons may be held liable for environmental offences committed in the course of their operations.

6. The court may order remediation and restoration of environmental damage, confiscation of proceeds and instrumentalities of environmental crime, and prohibition from engaging in activities that pose environmental risks.

7. This Article applies to environmental offences committed by nationals or Verified Kaharagians anywhere in the world, and to offences affecting environmental interests or resources in which the State has rights or interests.
