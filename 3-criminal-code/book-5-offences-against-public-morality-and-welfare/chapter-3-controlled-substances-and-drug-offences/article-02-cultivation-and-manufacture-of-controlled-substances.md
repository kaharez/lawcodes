# Article 2 â€“ Cultivation and Manufacture of Controlled Substances

1. Cultivation and manufacture of controlled substances is the growing, producing, manufacturing, extracting, preparing, compounding, or processing of any controlled substance without lawful authorization.

2. Cultivation and manufacture includes:
    a. cultivating cannabis plants or other plants containing controlled substances;
    b. manufacturing synthetic drugs, including amphetamines, methamphetamines, or designer drugs;
    c. extracting or processing controlled substances from plant materials or precursor chemicals;
    d. operating clandestine laboratories or facilities for drug production;
    e. compounding or preparing controlled substances for distribution;
    f. producing or synthesizing new psychoactive substances; or
    g. any other act of creating or producing controlled substances.

3. Cultivation and manufacture of controlled substances is punished under Penalty Band E.

4. The penalty is aggravated where:
    a. the cultivation or manufacture involves substantial quantities;
    b. the cultivation or manufacture is part of organized criminal activity or trafficking networks;
    c. the cultivation or manufacture involves particularly dangerous or harmful substances;
    d. the cultivation or manufacture creates environmental hazards, fire risks, or public safety dangers;
    e. the cultivation or manufacture occurs in residential areas or near schools, endangering others;
    f. the cultivation or manufacture involves children or vulnerable persons in the operations;
    g. the offender holds a professional or official position of trust; or
    h. the offender has previous convictions for drug offences.

5. Possession of equipment, materials, or precursor chemicals for the purpose of manufacturing controlled substances is punished under Penalty Band D.

6. Small-scale cultivation of cannabis plants for personal use may be subject to reduced penalties under Penalty Band B or C, depending on the quantity and circumstances, where decriminalization policies apply.

7. The court shall order destruction of controlled substances, confiscation of equipment and facilities, and confiscation of proceeds of manufacture.

8. Juridical persons may be held liable for cultivation and manufacture of controlled substances conducted through their operations or premises.
