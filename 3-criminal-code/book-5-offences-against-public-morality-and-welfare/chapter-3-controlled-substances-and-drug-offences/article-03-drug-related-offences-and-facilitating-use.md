# Article 3 â€“ Drug-Related Offences and Facilitating Use

1. It is an offence to operate, maintain, or permit premises or facilities to be used for the unlawful production, supply, or consumption of controlled substances.

2. Facilitating drug use and related offences include:
    a. operating drug houses, crack houses, or premises for drug use;
    b. permitting premises or property to be used for drug production, storage, or trafficking;
    c. operating facilities or venues where controlled substances are openly consumed or distributed;
    d. providing equipment, facilities, or premises for drug use or production;
    e. facilitating or organizing drug use events or gatherings;
    f. supplying drug paraphernalia for the purpose of facilitating unlawful drug use; or
    g. knowingly allowing property under one's control to be used for drug offences.

3. Operating or maintaining drug premises is punished under Penalty Band D.

4. The penalty is aggravated to Penalty Band E where:
    a. the premises are operated systematically or commercially;
    b. the premises facilitate trafficking or large-scale drug use;
    c. the premises are located near schools, youth facilities, or residential areas;
    d. minors are permitted access to the premises;
    e. the premises create public health or safety hazards;
    f. the operation involves violence, coercion, or exploitation; or
    g. the offender has previous convictions for drug offences.

5. Permitting premises to be used for drug offences by persons in control of property (owners, landlords, occupiers) is punished under Penalty Band C, or Penalty Band D where the person knowingly permits systematic use.

6. Supply of drug paraphernalia, including pipes, syringes, or equipment specifically designed for drug use, with knowledge or intent that it be used for unlawful drug consumption, is punished under Penalty Band B.

7. It is a defence if:
    a. the premises or facilities are operated for legitimate harm reduction, medical, or public health purposes, including supervised consumption sites or needle exchange programs authorized by law;
    b. the person did not know and had no reasonable grounds to suspect that the premises were being used for drug offences; or
    c. the person took reasonable steps to prevent such use upon becoming aware of it.

8. The court may order closure of premises, confiscation of property, and prohibition from operating or leasing premises for specified periods.

9. Harm reduction services, including provision of clean needles, drug checking services, or supervised consumption sites, are not offences under this Article where operated in accordance with public health policies and authorized by competent authorities.
