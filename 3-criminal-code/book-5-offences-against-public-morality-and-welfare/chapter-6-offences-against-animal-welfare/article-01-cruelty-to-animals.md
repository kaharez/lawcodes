# Article 1 â€“ Cruelty to Animals

1. Cruelty to animals is the intentional or reckless infliction of pain, suffering, injury, or distress upon an animal, or the commission of any act that causes unnecessary suffering to an animal.

2. Cruelty to animals includes:
    (a) beating, kicking, striking, or otherwise physically abusing an animal;
    (b) torturing, tormenting, or terrorising an animal;
    (c) causing or procuring fights between animals for entertainment or gain;
    (d) using animals for cruel entertainment or practices;
    (e) subjecting animals to unnecessary pain during training, work, or handling;
    (f) confining animals in conditions that cause severe physical or psychological suffering;
    (g) poisoning animals or administering harmful substances without veterinary justification;
    (h) performing or procuring surgical or invasive procedures without proper anaesthesia, veterinary justification, or in violation of professional standards;
    (i) overworking, overloading, or driving animals beyond their capacity causing suffering or injury;
    (j) using cruel or inhumane methods of capture, restraint, transport, or slaughter; or
    (k) any other act that intentionally or recklessly causes unnecessary pain, suffering, or distress to an animal.

3. Cruelty to animals is punished under Penalty Band C.

4. The penalty is aggravated to Penalty Band D where:
    (a) the cruelty is severe, prolonged, or systematic;
    (b) the cruelty results in serious injury, permanent disability, or death of the animal;
    (c) the cruelty is committed against multiple animals;
    (d) the cruelty is committed for commercial purposes or entertainment;
    (e) the cruelty is committed by a person in a position of trust or professional responsibility over animals;
    (f) the cruelty is committed in the presence of children or for the purpose of threatening or intimidating persons;
    (g) the offender has previous convictions for animal cruelty or related offences; or
    (h) the cruelty involves endangered, protected, or particularly vulnerable species.

5. The penalty is aggravated to Penalty Band E where the cruelty involves:
    (a) torture, sadistic conduct, or extreme gratuitous violence causing prolonged and severe suffering;
    (b) systematic torture or cruelty conducted over an extended period;
    (c) cruelty resulting in particularly gruesome or painful deaths;
    (d) cruelty committed for sexual gratification or perverse pleasure;
    (e) organized or commercial systematic cruelty involving multiple animals; or
    (f) cruelty of such extreme nature as to shock the public conscience.

6. It is not a defence that:
    (a) the animal was owned by the accused;
    (b) the conduct was part of traditional, cultural, or religious practice;
    (c) the conduct was intended as discipline or training; or
    (d) the animal was destined for slaughter or other lawful purpose.

7. It is a defence that:
    (a) the conduct was necessary to prevent immediate danger to human life or safety;
    (b) the conduct was carried out by a qualified veterinarian in accordance with professional standards for legitimate medical purposes;
    (c) the conduct was authorised by law for legitimate purposes and carried out humanely in accordance with applicable regulations; or
    (d) the accused honestly and reasonably believed the conduct was necessary to relieve the animal's suffering and acted in good faith in the animal's best interests.

8. The court may order:
    (a) permanent or temporary prohibition from owning, keeping, or working with animals;
    (b) confiscation of animals in the offender's possession;
    (c) payment of veterinary costs and compensation for harm to animals;
    (d) community service related to animal welfare; or
    (e) mandatory education or treatment regarding animal welfare.

9. Where cruelty to animals is committed as part of domestic violence, intimidation, or coercion of persons, the offence may also constitute offences under Book III, Chapter 6, and the animal cruelty shall be considered an aggravating circumstance.
