# Article 7 â€“ Unlawful Breeding and Trade

## Failure to Register Intact Animals

1. Failure to register intact companion animals as required by the Civil Code, Book III, Chapter 8, Article 6 is an offence.

2. Registration offences include:
    (a) keeping intact animals without registration;
    (b) providing false or misleading information in registration;
    (c) failing to renew registration as required;
    (d) failing to update registration when circumstances change; or
    (e) obstructing or impeding registration enforcement.

3. Failure to register intact animals is punished under Penalty Band A for first offences, or Penalty Band B for repeated violations.

4. Where failure to register results in uncontrolled breeding, the penalty is aggravated to Penalty Band C.

## Prohibited Breeding Practices

5. Engaging in prohibited breeding practices in violation of the Civil Code, Book III, Chapter 8, Article 6 is an offence.

6. Prohibited breeding practices include:
    (a) breeding animals with known genetic disorders or hereditary diseases;
    (b) breeding for extreme conformational traits causing suffering (such as severe brachycephaly, skin folds, skeletal deformities);
    (c) inbreeding or line breeding causing genetic disorders;
    (d) breeding animals too young, too old, or too frequently;
    (e) breeding without adequate veterinary oversight;
    (f) operating puppy mills, kitten mills, or other high-volume, low-welfare breeding operations;
    (g) breeding wild or exotic animals for pet trade except under conservation programmes;
    (h) breeding animals for fighting, cruel entertainment, or prohibited purposes; or
    (i) any breeding practice prioritising profit over animal welfare.

7. Prohibited breeding practices are punished under Penalty Band C.

8. The penalty is aggravated to Penalty Band D where:
    (a) breeding is conducted on a commercial scale;
    (b) breeding affects large numbers of animals;
    (c) breeding causes severe suffering, deformity, or death;
    (d) the offender operates a puppy mill or similar facility;
    (e) the offender conceals or misrepresents breeding practices;
    (f) the offender continues breeding despite warnings;
    (g) the offender has previous convictions for animal welfare offences; or
    (h) breeding is for prohibited purposes such as animal fighting.

## Unlicensed Commercial Breeding

9. Operating a commercial breeding operation without required licences and permits is an offence.

10. Unlicensed commercial breeding includes:
    (a) breeding animals for sale without proper licensing;
    (b) operating breeding facilities without registration;
    (c) breeding beyond the scale permitted for casual ownership without commercial licence;
    (d) violating conditions of breeding licences;
    (e) failing to maintain required records;
    (f) failing to submit to required inspections; or
    (g) operating in violation of regulations.

11. Unlicensed commercial breeding is punished under Penalty Band C, or Penalty Band D where conducted on a large scale or with aggravating circumstances.

## Puppy Mills and Substandard Breeding Operations

12. Operating puppy mills, kitten mills, or other high-volume, low-welfare breeding operations is an aggravated offence.

13. Characteristics of puppy mills and substandard operations include:
    (a) breeding large numbers of animals in inadequate conditions;
    (b) prioritising profit over animal welfare;
    (c) keeping breeding animals in cruel confinement;
    (d) failing to provide adequate veterinary care, socialisation, or humane conditions;
    (e) breeding animals excessively or continuously;
    (f) separating offspring from mothers prematurely;
    (g) selling animals without proper health screening or care;
    (h) operating without transparency or accountability; or
    (i) any combination of practices constituting systematic animal cruelty for profit.

14. Operating puppy mills or substandard breeding operations is punished under Penalty Band E, with mandatory:
    (a) permanent closure of the operation;
    (b) permanent prohibition from animal breeding or trade;
    (c) confiscation of all animals;
    (d) payment for lifetime care of rescued animals; and
    (e) publication of the conviction.

## Unlawful Sales and Trade

15. Selling, transferring, or trading animals in violation of the Civil Code, Book III, Chapter 8, Article 6 is an offence.

16. Unlawful sales and trade include:
    (a) selling animals without proper identification or health records;
    (b) selling animals below minimum age requirements;
    (c) selling animals sourced from puppy mills or illegal breeders;
    (d) selling animals in prohibited venues or platforms;
    (e) selling animals without verifying buyer capability;
    (f) misrepresenting breeding conditions, health, or parentage;
    (g) failing to provide required information to buyers;
    (h) violating consumer protection or animal welfare requirements; or
    (i) selling animals from prohibited sources.

17. Unlawful sales are punished under Penalty Band B, or Penalty Band C where conducted commercially or with aggravating circumstances.

18. Operating unlicensed pet stores or animal retail operations is punished under Penalty Band C.

19. Selling animals sourced from puppy mills or knowing they were bred in cruel conditions is punished under Penalty Band D.

## Import and Export Violations

20. Importing or exporting animals in violation of regulations or from prohibited sources is an offence.

21. Import and export violations include:
    (a) importing animals from jurisdictions with lower welfare standards;
    (b) importing animals bred in prohibited conditions;
    (c) trafficking in animals from illegal sources;
    (d) importing or exporting animals for prohibited purposes;
    (e) violating quarantine, health, or documentation requirements;
    (f) smuggling animals; or
    (g) any trade in animals violating welfare or conservation laws.

22. Import and export violations are punished under Penalty Band C, or Penalty Band D where involving endangered species, large numbers, or commercial operations.

## Online and Platform Sales

23. Selling or advertising animals through online platforms, social media, or classified advertisements in violation of regulations is an offence.

24. Platforms and websites that facilitate unlawful animal sales may be subject to:
    (a) orders to remove listings and prevent further violations;
    (b) fines for failing to monitor or remove illegal content;
    (c) liability for facilitating illegal trade; or
    (d) blocking or prohibition of operations in Kaharagia.

25. Online sales violations are punished under Penalty Band B, or Penalty Band C where conducted on a commercial scale.

## Violations by Shelters and Rescue Organisations

26. Animal shelters or rescue organisations that:
    (a) fail to spay or neuter animals before adoption without medical justification;
    (b) operate without proper licensing or inspection;
    (c) misrepresent animals' health, behaviour, or history;
    (d) fail to screen adopters appropriately;
    (e) engage in profit-driven rather than welfare-driven activities;
    (f) maintain inadequate conditions for animals;
    (g) operate fraudulently or deceptively; or
    (h) violate regulations applicable to shelters and rescues;

are guilty of offences punishable under Penalty Band C, with potential revocation of licences and closure.

## General Provisions

27. It is not a defence that:
    (a) breeding or trade practices are common in the industry;
    (b) practices are legal in other jurisdictions;
    (c) buyers requested or consented to substandard practices;
    (d) practices are profitable;
    (e) the accused was unaware of specific requirements; or
    (f) animals appeared healthy despite breeding problems.

28. It is a defence that:
    (a) the accused was operating under a valid licence and in full compliance with conditions;
    (b) breeding was conducted by accredited conservation programmes for legitimate purposes;
    (c) the accused took all reasonable steps to comply and reasonably believed they were in compliance;
    (d) registration failure was inadvertent, immediately corrected, and caused no harm; or
    (e) the accused was in a recognised transition period and complying with transition requirements.

29. The court may order:
    (a) permanent prohibition from breeding, selling, or keeping animals;
    (b) confiscation of animals and transfer to approved care;
    (c) revocation of licences and closure of operations;
    (d) payment for care and rehabilitation of animals;
    (e) restitution to buyers or affected persons;
    (f) publication of convictions;
    (g) prohibition from online sales or advertising;
    (h) mandatory spaying or neutering of animals at offender's expense;
    (i) forfeiture of proceeds from illegal breeding or sales; or
    (j) any other remedy appropriate to protect animals and prevent future violations.

30. Where offences are committed by commercial breeders or pet stores:
    (a) the business may be permanently closed;
    (b) operators may be permanently prohibited from animal-related businesses;
    (c) all animals must be confiscated;
    (d) proceeds must be forfeited; and
    (e) criminal liability extends to owners, managers, and responsible persons.

31. Persons who purchase animals from known puppy mills or illegal sources may be subject to:
    (a) confiscation of animals;
    (b) fines under Penalty Band A; and
    (c) education requirements regarding responsible acquisition.

32. This Article gives effect to the breeding and trade regulations established in the Civil Code, Book III, Chapter 8, Article 6, and violations may constitute both civil and criminal liability.

33. Enforcement authorities shall:
    (a) maintain and enforce registration systems;
    (b) inspect and regulate breeding operations;
    (c) investigate and prosecute illegal breeding and trade;
    (d) rescue and rehome animals from illegal operations;
    (e) educate the public about responsible acquisition;
    (f) work with platforms to prevent illegal sales;
    (g) coordinate with other jurisdictions to prevent trafficking; and
    (h) support responsible breeders and welfare organisations.

34. The Sovereign may, by Sovereign Decree, establish:
    (a) specific penalties and enforcement mechanisms;
    (b) licensing and registration systems;
    (c) standards for breeding and trade;
    (d) transitional provisions for existing operations;
    (e) coordination with local authorities; and
    (f) any other measures necessary to implement these provisions.
