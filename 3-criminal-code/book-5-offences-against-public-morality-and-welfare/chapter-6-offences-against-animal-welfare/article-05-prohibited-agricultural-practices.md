# Article 5 â€“ Prohibited Agricultural Practices

1. This Article addresses prohibited practices in agricultural and food production contexts. For prohibited mutilations of companion and pet animals, see Article 4 of this Chapter.

2. Operating, maintaining, or using battery cages or prohibited intensive confinement systems in violation of the Civil Code, Book III, Chapter 8, Article 4 is an offence.

3. Prohibited confinement practices include:
    (a) using battery cages for poultry or any animals;
    (b) using gestation or farrowing crates for pigs;
    (c) using veal crates or individual calf confinement;
    (d) confining animals in systems that prevent natural behaviours;
    (e) maintaining conditions that cause injury, deformity, or chronic suffering; or
    (f) any other intensive confinement system prohibited by law or regulation.

4. Prohibited confinement practices are punished under Penalty Band C.

5. Performing prohibited mutilations on agricultural animals is an offence, including:
    (a) beak trimming or debeaking of poultry for non-therapeutic purposes;
    (b) tail docking except for documented medical necessity;
    (c) tooth clipping or grinding without appropriate anaesthesia;
    (d) castration, dehorning, or disbudding without appropriate anaesthesia and analgesia;
    (e) any cosmetic or convenience mutilation; or
    (f) any procedure prohibited by the Civil Code or regulation.

6. Prohibited agricultural mutilations are punished under Penalty Band C, or Penalty Band D where performed systematically or causing severe suffering.

7. Chick culling and live maceration are offences, including:
    (a) killing day-old chicks by any method;
    (b) live maceration or grinding of birds;
    (c) killing newly hatched birds for sex or commercial selection;
    (d) disposing of live birds as waste; or
    (e) using inhumane methods to kill young birds.

8. Chick culling is punished under Penalty Band C, or Penalty Band D where conducted systematically or on a commercial scale.

9. Foie gras production and trade are offences, including:
    (a) force-feeding birds to produce foie gras;
    (b) operating facilities for foie gras production;
    (c) importing foie gras produced by force-feeding;
    (d) selling or distributing foie gras produced by force-feeding;
    (e) serving foie gras in restaurants or food establishments; or
    (f) advertising or promoting foie gras produced by force-feeding.

10. Foie gras offences are punished under Penalty Band D.

11. The penalty is aggravated to Penalty Band E where:
    (a) prohibited practices are conducted on a commercial or industrial scale;
    (b) prohibited practices are systematic and prolonged;
    (c) prohibited practices affect large numbers of animals;
    (d) the offender operates a business or facility engaged in prohibited practices;
    (e) the offender conceals or misrepresents practices;
    (f) the offender continues prohibited practices despite warnings or enforcement action;
    (g) the offender profits substantially from prohibited practices; or
    (h) the offender has previous convictions for animal welfare offences.

12. Performing ritual slaughter without authorisation is an offence, including:
    (a) slaughtering animals without prior stunning except where authorised by special licence from The Sovereign;
    (b) violating conditions of any special licence granted;
    (c) performing slaughter methods causing unnecessary suffering;
    (d) operating unauthorised slaughter facilities; or
    (e) failing to comply with welfare requirements for any authorised ritual slaughter.

13. Unauthorised ritual slaughter is punished under Penalty Band D, or Penalty Band E where conducted commercially or systematically.

14. It is not a defence that:
    (a) the prohibited practices are customary, traditional, or common in the industry;
    (b) the prohibited practices are legally permitted in other jurisdictions;
    (c) alternatives would be more costly or less profitable;
    (d) the practices comply with lower standards applicable elsewhere;
    (e) other producers or competitors engage in the same practices; or
    (f) industry representatives advocate for the practices.

15. It is a defence that:
    (a) the accused was in a transition period established by regulation and was complying with transition requirements;
    (b) the practice was specifically authorised by Sovereign Decree or special licence and conducted in compliance with all conditions;
    (c) the practice was performed by a veterinarian for documented therapeutic medical necessity; or
    (d) the accused reasonably believed they were in compliance based on official guidance and took reasonable steps to ensure compliance.

16. The court may order:
    (a) closure of facilities and prohibition from animal agriculture;
    (b) confiscation of animals and transfer to appropriate care;
    (c) payment of costs for animal care and rehabilitation;
    (d) destruction or modification of prohibited equipment or facilities;
    (e) publication of the conviction;
    (f) restitution to animals harmed; or
    (g) any other remedy appropriate to prevent continued violations.

17. Commercial operators engaged in prohibited practices must be shut down and prohibited from operating in animal agriculture.

18. Import, export, or trade in products from prohibited practices is punished under Penalty Band C, or Penalty Band D where conducted commercially.

19. This Article gives effect to the prohibitions established in the Civil Code, Book III, Chapter 8, Article 4, and violations constitute both civil and criminal liability.
