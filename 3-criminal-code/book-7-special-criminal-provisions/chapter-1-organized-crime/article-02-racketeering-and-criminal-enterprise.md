# Article 2 â€“ Racketeering and Criminal Enterprise

1. Racketeering is the systematic commission of offences or engagement in criminal activities as part of an ongoing criminal enterprise for the purpose of generating income or achieving economic or political objectives.

2. Racketeering and criminal enterprise includes:
    a. conducting a pattern of criminal activity through an enterprise or organisation;
    b. acquiring or maintaining control of legitimate businesses through criminal means;
    c. operating criminal enterprises, including through extortion, fraud, money laundering, or illegal trade;
    d. using legitimate businesses as fronts for criminal activity;
    e. investing proceeds of crime in legitimate businesses or enterprises;
    f. systematic extortion, protection rackets, or illegal taxation;
    g. controlling or monopolizing illegal markets or activities; or
    h. any systematic criminal activity conducted through an ongoing enterprise.

3. For the purposes of this Article:
    a. "pattern of criminal activity" means the commission of at least two predicate offences within a five-year period;
    b. "predicate offences" include any offences punishable under Penalty Band D or E, including but not limited to trafficking, fraud, extortion, money laundering, corruption, and violence;
    c. "enterprise" includes any individual, partnership, corporation, association, or group of persons associated in fact, whether formal or informal; and
    d. it is not necessary that the enterprise has a legitimate purpose or lawful existence.

4. Racketeering is punished under Penalty Band E.

5. The penalty is aggravated where:
    a. the racketeering enterprise is large-scale, sophisticated, or operates transnationally;
    b. the racketeering involves serious violence, corruption of officials, or infiltration of government;
    c. the racketeering causes substantial economic harm or undermines legitimate business;
    d. the racketeering involves multiple predicate offences or systematic criminal activity over an extended period;
    e. the offender holds a leadership or management position in the criminal enterprise;
    f. the racketeering enterprise has corrupted or infiltrated legitimate institutions; or
    g. the offender has previous convictions for organized crime or racketeering.

6. The court shall order confiscation of all assets, proceeds, and property of the criminal enterprise, including forfeiture of any interest in legitimate businesses acquired or maintained through racketeering.

7. Juridical persons may be dissolved and their assets forfeited where they are substantially controlled by or operated for racketeering purposes.

8. The court may recommend to The Sovereign that loss of nationality be considered for leaders or senior members of racketeering enterprises engaged in grave criminal activity.

9. This Article applies to racketeering activity conducted anywhere in the world by nationals or Verified Kaharagians, or affecting Kaharagian interests.
