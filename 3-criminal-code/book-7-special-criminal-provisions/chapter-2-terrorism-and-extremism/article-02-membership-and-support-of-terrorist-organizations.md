# Article 2 â€“ Membership and Support of Terrorist Organizations

1. A terrorist organisation is any structured group that engages in, prepares, or advocates terrorist acts as defined in Article 1 of this Chapter, or that has as its purpose the commission of terrorist acts.

2. The following acts constitute offences:
    a. founding, establishing, or directing a terrorist organisation (Penalty Band E, with mandatory minimum penalties);
    b. being a member of or participating in a terrorist organisation (Penalty Band E);
    c. recruiting members for a terrorist organisation (Penalty Band E);
    d. training or receiving training for terrorist purposes (Penalty Band E);
    e. financing or providing material support to a terrorist organisation (Penalty Band E);
    f. providing weapons, explosives, or dangerous materials to a terrorist organisation (Penalty Band E);
    g. providing facilities, safe harbour, or logistical support to terrorists or terrorist organisations (Penalty Band D);
    h. disseminating propaganda or material in support of terrorism or terrorist organisations (Penalty Band D); or
    i. traveling to join or support a terrorist organisation (Penalty Band D).

3. For the purposes of this Article:
    a. material support includes financial resources, property, services, training, expert advice, facilities, communications equipment, weapons, or any other assistance;
    b. membership includes formal membership, association, or participation in the activities or objectives of the organisation; and
    c. it is not necessary that the organisation has committed a terrorist act, only that its purpose or activities involve preparation for or advocacy of terrorism.

4. It is not a defence that:
    a. the accused did not personally commit or plan to commit a terrorist act;
    b. the accused supported the organisation for humanitarian, political, or ideological reasons;
    c. the support provided was non-violent or indirect; or
    d. the accused's participation or support occurred outside the territory of any State.

5. The court shall order confiscation of all assets and property used for or derived from support of terrorism.

6. The court shall recommend to The Sovereign that loss of nationality be considered in all cases of membership or material support of terrorist organisations.

7. Victims or persons coerced into association with terrorist organisations may raise coercion or duress as a defence, subject to proof that they had no reasonable opportunity to escape or refuse, and that they did not voluntarily participate in terrorist activities.
