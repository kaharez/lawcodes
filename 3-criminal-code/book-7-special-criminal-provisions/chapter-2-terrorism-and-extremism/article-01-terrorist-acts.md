# Article 1 â€“ Terrorist Acts

1. A terrorist act is any act committed with the intention of:
    (a) causing death or serious bodily injury to civilians or non-combatants;
    (b) causing extensive destruction or serious damage to property, infrastructure, or natural resources where such destruction causes or is likely to cause major economic loss or serious danger to human life or safety;
    (c) endangering life, creating a serious risk to public health or safety, or causing serious interference with or disruption to essential services, infrastructure, or electronic systems;
    (d) intimidating a population or compelling a government, international organisation, or person to do or abstain from doing any act; or
    (e) seriously destabilizing or destroying fundamental political, constitutional, economic, or social structures of a State or international organisation.

2. Terrorist acts include:
    (a) attacks causing death or injury to persons or destruction of property;
    (b) taking or holding hostages;
    (c) hijacking or seizure of aircraft, vessels, vehicles, or other means of transport;
    (d) use of explosives, firearms, chemical, biological, radiological, or nuclear weapons or materials;
    (e) attacks on critical infrastructure, including transport, communication, energy, water, or health systems;
    (f) cyberterrorism, including attacks on digital infrastructure or systems for terrorist purposes;
    (g) dissemination of dangerous substances or causing catastrophic environmental damage for terrorist purposes; or
    (h) any other act intended to cause death, serious injury, or extensive destruction for the purposes set out in paragraph 1.

3. Terrorist acts are punished under Penalty Band E, with mandatory minimum penalties and without possibility of pardon or reduction except by Sovereign decree.

4. The penalty is aggravated where:
    (a) the terrorist act results in death or mass casualties;
    (b) the terrorist act causes catastrophic destruction or disruption;
    (c) the terrorist act uses weapons of mass destruction or particularly dangerous means;
    (d) the terrorist act targets civilians, protected persons, or particularly vulnerable groups;
    (e) the terrorist act is part of systematic or coordinated terrorist activity; or
    (f) the offender holds a position of leadership in a terrorist organisation.

5. Attempted terrorist acts are punished under Penalty Band E.

6. The court shall order confiscation of all property and assets used in or derived from terrorist activity.

7. The court shall recommend to The Sovereign that loss of nationality be considered in all cases of conviction for terrorist acts.

8. This Article applies to terrorist acts committed anywhere in the world by nationals or Verified Kaharagians, or against the interests, property, or nationals of the State.
