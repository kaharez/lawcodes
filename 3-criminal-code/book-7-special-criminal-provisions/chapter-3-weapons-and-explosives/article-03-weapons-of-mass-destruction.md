# Article 3 â€“ Weapons of Mass Destruction

1. It is an offence to develop, manufacture, acquire, possess, stockpile, transfer, or use chemical, biological, radiological, or nuclear weapons or materials, or weapons designed to cause mass casualties or widespread destruction.

2. For the purposes of this Article, weapons of mass destruction include:
    (a) chemical weapons, including toxic chemicals and their precursors intended for hostile purposes;
    (b) biological weapons, including biological agents, toxins, or delivery systems intended for hostile purposes;
    (c) radiological weapons or devices designed to disperse radioactive materials;
    (d) nuclear weapons, nuclear explosive devices, or fissile materials intended for weapons purposes;
    (e) any weapon or device designed to release radiation, radioactivity, or radioactive materials;
    (f) any weapon designed to cause indiscriminate mass casualties or destruction; or
    (g) delivery systems, components, or equipment specifically designed for weapons of mass destruction.

3. Any act involving weapons of mass destruction is punished under Penalty Band E, with mandatory life imprisonment or the most severe penalty available.

4. Specific offences include:
    (a) developing, manufacturing, or producing weapons of mass destruction (Penalty Band E, mandatory life imprisonment);
    (b) acquiring, possessing, or stockpiling weapons of mass destruction or their components (Penalty Band E);
    (c) transferring, supplying, or assisting others to acquire weapons of mass destruction (Penalty Band E);
    (d) using or threatening to use weapons of mass destruction (Penalty Band E, mandatory life imprisonment);
    (e) financing or facilitating the development or acquisition of weapons of mass destruction (Penalty Band E);
    (f) providing scientific, technical, or expert assistance for weapons of mass destruction programs (Penalty Band E); or
    (g) attempting or conspiring to commit any of the foregoing acts (Penalty Band E).

5. There are no mitigating circumstances for offences involving weapons of mass destruction, and penalties shall reflect the extreme gravity and danger of such weapons.

6. The court shall recommend to The Sovereign that loss of nationality be considered in all cases involving weapons of mass destruction.

7. The court shall order destruction of weapons, materials, and equipment under international supervision, and confiscation of all assets connected to weapons of mass destruction programs.

8. This Article gives effect to Kaharagia's obligations under:
    (a) the Chemical Weapons Convention;
    (b) the Biological Weapons Convention;
    (c) the Treaty on the Non-Proliferation of Nuclear Weapons;
    (d) customary international law prohibiting weapons of mass destruction; and
    (e) any other international agreements on disarmament and non-proliferation.

9. The State shall exercise universal jurisdiction over offences involving weapons of mass destruction committed by any person anywhere in the world, and shall cooperate fully with international organizations and other States in preventing proliferation and prosecuting offenders.

10. Scientific research for peaceful purposes, medical applications, or legitimate industrial uses of chemical, biological, or nuclear materials is not prohibited, provided such activities are conducted transparently, under appropriate safeguards, and in compliance with international obligations.
