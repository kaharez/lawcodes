# Article 1 â€“ Illegal Possession and Trafficking of Weapons

1. It is an offence to manufacture, possess, carry, transfer, import, export, or traffic in prohibited weapons, explosives, or dangerous devices without lawful authorization.

2. For the purposes of this Article, prohibited weapons and items include:
    (a) military-grade firearms, automatic weapons, and weapons of war;
    (b) explosives, explosive devices, and incendiary devices;
    (c) chemical, biological, radiological, or nuclear weapons or materials;
    (d) weapons or devices designed to cause indiscriminate harm or destruction;
    (e) component parts and materials specifically designed for manufacturing weapons or explosives; and
    (f) any other weapons or items designated as prohibited by law or regulation.

3. The following acts constitute offences:
    (a) manufacturing or producing prohibited weapons or explosives (Penalty Band E);
    (b) trafficking, importing, or exporting prohibited weapons or explosives (Penalty Band E);
    (c) possessing prohibited weapons or explosives (Penalty Band D);
    (d) transferring or supplying prohibited weapons or explosives to another person (Penalty Band E);
    (e) using prohibited weapons or explosives in the commission of any offence (Penalty Band E, in addition to penalties for the underlying offence); or
    (f) modifying legal weapons to create prohibited weapons or increasing their lethality (Penalty Band D).

4. The penalty is aggravated where:
    (a) the weapons or explosives are used or intended for use in terrorist acts, organized crime, or other serious offences;
    (b) the trafficking or possession involves substantial quantities;
    (c) the offence involves transnational trafficking or organized criminal networks;
    (d) the weapons or explosives are transferred to prohibited persons, including minors or persons with criminal backgrounds;
    (e) the offence involves weapons of mass destruction or particularly dangerous materials; or
    (f) the offender has previous convictions for weapons offences.

5. Legal possession and use of weapons for legitimate purposes, including lawful sporting, hunting, collecting, or security purposes, is not prohibited, provided proper licensing, registration, and storage requirements are met in accordance with applicable laws and regulations.

6. The court may order confiscation and destruction of prohibited weapons and explosives, and may impose permanent prohibition from possessing or dealing in weapons.

7. This Article applies to offences committed anywhere in the world by nationals or Verified Kaharagians.
