# Article 2 â€“ Manufacture and Supply of Explosives

1. It is an offence to manufacture, assemble, supply, transfer, import, export, or possess explosives, explosive devices, or incendiary devices without lawful authorization.

2. Manufacture and supply of explosives includes:
    a. manufacturing or producing explosives, explosive devices, or bomb-making materials;
    b. assembling or constructing explosive or incendiary devices;
    c. supplying, transferring, or distributing explosives or explosive devices to unauthorized persons;
    d. importing or exporting explosives or explosive materials without proper licensing;
    e. possessing explosives or explosive precursors for unlawful purposes;
    f. providing instructions, formulas, or guidance for manufacturing explosives or bombs;
    g. operating illegal explosives manufacturing facilities; or
    h. any other unlawful activity involving explosives or explosive materials.

3. Manufacture of explosives or explosive devices is punished under Penalty Band E.

4. Supply or transfer of explosives to unauthorized persons is punished under Penalty Band E.

5. Unlawful possession of explosives or explosive devices is punished under Penalty Band D.

6. The penalty is aggravated where:
    a. the explosives or devices are intended for use in terrorist acts, organized crime, or serious violence;
    b. the explosives or devices are of substantial quantity or destructive capacity;
    c. the manufacture or supply is systematic or commercial;
    d. the explosives or devices are supplied to prohibited persons, including terrorists or criminals;
    e. the manufacture or possession creates substantial danger to public safety;
    f. the offender has previous convictions for weapons or explosives offences; or
    g. the explosives or devices are actually used in the commission of offences.

7. Dissemination of bomb-making instructions, manuals, or formulas with intent to facilitate the unlawful manufacture of explosives is punished under Penalty Band C, or Penalty Band D where the instructions are specifically designed for terrorist purposes or mass harm.

8. Lawful manufacture, possession, and use of explosives for legitimate purposes, including mining, construction, demolition, and pyrotechnics, is not prohibited provided proper licensing, storage, and safety requirements are met in accordance with law.

9. The court shall order confiscation and destruction of unlawful explosives, devices, and materials, and may impose permanent prohibition from possessing or dealing in explosives.

10. This Article applies to offences committed anywhere in the world by nationals or Verified Kaharagians.
