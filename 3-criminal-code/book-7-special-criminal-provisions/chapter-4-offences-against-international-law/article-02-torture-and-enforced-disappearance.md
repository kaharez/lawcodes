# Article 2 â€“ Torture and Enforced Disappearance

1. Torture is any act by which severe pain or suffering, whether physical or mental, is intentionally inflicted on a person for purposes of obtaining information or a confession, punishment, intimidation, coercion, or for any reason based on discrimination of any kind.

2. Torture includes:
    a. inflicting severe physical pain or injury;
    b. inflicting severe mental pain or suffering through psychological methods;
    c. subjecting persons to cruel, inhuman, or degrading treatment;
    d. using methods designed to break the will or destroy the personality;
    e. inflicting pain or suffering by or at the instigation of or with the acquiescence of public officials or persons acting in official capacity; or
    f. torture committed in the context of armed conflict, detention, or abuse of power.

3. Torture is punished under Penalty Band E, with mandatory severe penalties and no possibility of pardon except by Sovereign decree.

4. The penalty is aggravated where:
    a. the torture results in death, permanent injury, or severe physical or psychological harm;
    b. the torture is systematic or affects multiple victims;
    c. the torture involves particularly cruel or degrading methods;
    d. the victim is particularly vulnerable, including children, elderly persons, or persons with disabilities;
    e. the torture is committed by persons in positions of authority or public office;
    f. the torture is part of widespread or systematic attacks; or
    g. the offender has previous findings or convictions for torture or cruel treatment.

5. Enforced disappearance is the arrest, detention, abduction, or any other form of deprivation of liberty by or with the authorization, support, or acquiescence of the State or persons acting in official capacity, followed by a refusal to acknowledge the deprivation of liberty or to disclose the fate or whereabouts of the person.

6. Enforced disappearance is punished under Penalty Band E.

7. The penalty for enforced disappearance is aggravated where:
    a. the disappearance is prolonged or the person remains missing;
    b. the disappearance results in death or serious harm;
    c. the disappearance is systematic or affects multiple victims;
    d. the disappearance involves families, including children;
    e. the disappearance is part of widespread or systematic practice; or
    f. the offender holds senior official position or authority.

8. Superior orders, official capacity, or state of emergency are not defences to torture or enforced disappearance.

9. There is no statute of limitations for torture or enforced disappearance.

10. The court shall recommend to The Sovereign that loss of nationality be considered in all cases of torture or enforced disappearance.

11. The State shall exercise universal jurisdiction over torture and enforced disappearance committed by any person anywhere in the world.

12. This Article gives effect to Kaharagia's obligations under:
    a. the Convention Against Torture and Other Cruel, Inhuman or Degrading Treatment or Punishment;
    b. the International Convention for the Protection of All Persons from Enforced Disappearance;
    c. customary international law; and
    d. international humanitarian law.

13. Victims of torture and enforced disappearance shall have the right to rehabilitation, compensation, and truth about their suffering.
