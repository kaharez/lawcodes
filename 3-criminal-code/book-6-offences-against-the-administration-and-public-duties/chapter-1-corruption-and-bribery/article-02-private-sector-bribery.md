# Article 2 â€“ Private Sector Bribery

1. Private sector bribery is the offering, promising, giving, requesting, receiving, or accepting of any undue advantage in the course of economic, commercial, or professional activity, for the purpose of obtaining or retaining business, securing an improper advantage, or breaching duties of loyalty or good faith.

2. Private sector bribery includes:
    (a) bribing employees, agents, or representatives of private companies or organizations to breach their duties;
    (b) commercial bribery, including kickbacks, secret commissions, or corrupt payments in business transactions;
    (c) bribery in procurement, tendering, or contracting processes;
    (d) bribery of professional advisors, auditors, or persons in positions of trust;
    (e) bribery in sports, including match-fixing and corruption of athletes, officials, or administrators; or
    (f) any corrupt payment or advantage in private sector contexts.

3. Private sector bribery is punished under Penalty Band D.

4. The penalty is aggravated to Penalty Band E where:
    (a) the bribery involves substantial amounts or significant commercial advantage;
    (b) the bribery is systematic or part of organized criminal activity;
    (c) the bribery undermines fair competition or market integrity;
    (d) the bribery involves persons in senior positions or positions of significant trust;
    (e) the bribery facilitates money laundering, fraud, or other serious crimes; or
    (f) the offender has previous convictions for bribery or corruption.

5. Juridical persons may be held liable for private sector bribery committed by their directors, officers, employees, or agents.

6. The court may order confiscation of bribes and proceeds, disgorgement of profits, and disqualification from holding corporate or professional positions.
