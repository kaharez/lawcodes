# Article 3 â€“ Trading in Influence

1. Trading in influence is the act of offering, promising, giving, requesting, receiving, or accepting an undue advantage for the purpose of exerting improper influence over the decision-making of a public official or person in a position of authority.

2. Trading in influence includes:
    a. offering or receiving benefits in exchange for using one's influence, real or supposed, to affect decisions of public officials;
    b. acting as an intermediary in bribery schemes, facilitating corrupt payments between parties;
    c. soliciting or accepting benefits in exchange for promising to influence official decisions, even without authority to do so;
    d. offering benefits to persons close to public officials for the purpose of influencing those officials;
    e. leveraging political, social, or professional connections for corrupt purposes; or
    f. any other use of influence, real or perceived, for corrupt gain.

3. Trading in influence is punished under Penalty Band E.

4. The penalty is aggravated where:
    a. the influence trading concerns decisions of significant public importance;
    b. the influence trading involves substantial benefits or payments;
    c. the influence trading is systematic or part of organized corruption networks;
    d. the person holds or held public office or a position of authority;
    e. the influence trading undermines public confidence in institutions or governance;
    f. the influence trading facilitates other serious crimes; or
    g. the offender has previous convictions for corruption or bribery.

5. It is not a defence that:
    a. the person did not actually have influence over the official decision;
    b. the official decision was not ultimately affected;
    c. the influence was exerted through legitimate channels; or
    d. the benefit was provided after the decision was made.

6. Legitimate lobbying, advocacy, and representation conducted transparently and in accordance with law is not an offence under this Article.

7. The court shall order confiscation of all benefits and proceeds, and may impose disqualification from holding public office or positions of authority.

8. This Article gives effect to international anti-corruption standards, including the United Nations Convention Against Corruption.
