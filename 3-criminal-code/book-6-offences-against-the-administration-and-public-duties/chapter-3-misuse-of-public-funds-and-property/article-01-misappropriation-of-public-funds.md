# Article 1 â€“ Misappropriation of Public Funds

1. Misappropriation of public funds is the intentional and unlawful appropriation, diversion, or misuse of public money, funds, or financial resources by a person entrusted with their custody, administration, or disbursement.

2. Misappropriation of public funds includes:
    a. embezzling public money or funds;
    b. diverting public funds for unauthorized or improper purposes;
    c. making unauthorized or fraudulent expenditures of public funds;
    d. falsifying records or accounts to conceal misappropriation;
    e. awarding contracts, grants, or benefits improperly for personal or corrupt purposes;
    f. claiming false expenses, allowances, or entitlements from public funds; or
    g. any other dishonest appropriation or misuse of public financial resources.

3. Misappropriation of public funds is punished under Penalty Band E, with mandatory disqualification from holding public office.

4. The penalty is aggravated where:
    a. the amount misappropriated is very substantial;
    b. the misappropriation is systematic or prolonged;
    c. the misappropriation deprives the public of essential services or resources;
    d. the offender holds a senior position of responsibility for public finances;
    e. the misappropriation involves sophisticated schemes or falsification of records;
    f. the offender has previous convictions for misappropriation or corruption; or
    g. the misappropriation is committed in the context of emergency, crisis, or situations of public need.

5. The court may order full restitution of misappropriated funds, confiscation of proceeds and property acquired through misappropriation, and permanent disqualification from public service.

6. The court may recommend to The Sovereign that loss of nationality be considered in cases of grave misappropriation of public funds.

7. This Article applies to misappropriation of funds belonging to the State, public institutions, state-owned enterprises, or international organizations in which the State participates.
