# Article 2 â€“ Unauthorized Use of Public Property

1. Unauthorized use of public property is the use of public assets, resources, facilities, or services by a public official or other person for private purposes or in a manner not authorized by law.

2. Unauthorized use of public property includes:
    (a) using public vehicles, aircraft, vessels, or equipment for personal purposes;
    (b) using public facilities, buildings, or premises for private benefit;
    (c) using public employees, staff, or personnel for private work or services;
    (d) appropriating public supplies, materials, or resources for personal use;
    (e) using public telecommunications, IT systems, or services for unauthorized purposes;
    (f) allowing unauthorized persons to use public property or facilities;
    (g) converting public property to private use or benefit; or
    (h) any other unauthorized exploitation of public assets or resources.

3. Unauthorized use of public property is punished under Penalty Band C, with disqualification from holding public office where committed by public officials.

4. The penalty is aggravated to Penalty Band D where:
    (a) the unauthorized use is systematic, prolonged, or substantial in value;
    (b) the unauthorized use involves valuable assets or significant resources;
    (c) the unauthorized use is for commercial purposes or generates income;
    (d) the unauthorized use deprives the public of needed resources or services;
    (e) the official abused their position to conceal or facilitate the unauthorized use;
    (f) the official holds a senior position of responsibility for public property; or
    (g) the offender has previous convictions for misuse of public property or corruption.

5. Private persons who use public property without authorization, including through corruption, false pretenses, or trespass, are subject to the penalties under this Article.

6. The court may order payment for the value of use, restoration of property, and compensation for damage or loss.

7. Legitimate use of public property for official purposes, or de minimis personal use permitted by policy or custom (such as occasional personal phone calls), is not an offence under this Article.
