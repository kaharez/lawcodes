# Article 2 â€“ Corporate and Financial Fraud

1. Corporate and financial fraud includes fraudulent conduct in the context of business, commerce, financial services, or corporate governance.

2. Corporate and financial fraud includes:
    a. false accounting or falsification of financial records, statements, or reports;
    b. fraudulent misrepresentation in financial statements, prospectuses, or disclosures to investors, regulators, or the public;
    c. insider trading or trading on the basis of non-public material information;
    d. market manipulation, including manipulating prices, volumes, or market conditions for fraudulent purposes;
    e. fraudulent conduct in bankruptcy or insolvency proceedings, including concealing assets, making false claims, or preferring certain creditors fraudulently;
    f. operating a fraudulent business, including operating without proper authorization or for fraudulent purposes;
    g. fraudulent raising of capital or solicitation of investments;
    h. fraud in relation to cryptocurrencies, digital assets, or blockchain-based systems; or
    i. any other fraudulent conduct in commercial or financial contexts.

3. Corporate and financial fraud is punished under Penalty Band E.

4. The penalty is aggravated where:
    a. the fraud involves very substantial value or causes systemic financial harm;
    b. the fraud undermines market integrity or public confidence in financial systems;
    c. the fraud is committed by persons holding senior corporate, professional, or regulatory positions;
    d. the fraud involves sophisticated schemes or cross-border activity;
    e. the fraud affects pension funds, savings, or investments of vulnerable persons; or
    f. the fraud is part of organized criminal activity.

5. Juridical persons may be held liable for corporate and financial fraud committed by their directors, officers, employees, or agents, in accordance with the provisions of Book 1, Chapter 2, Article 4.

6. The court may order confiscation of proceeds, disgorgement of profits, restitution to victims, and prohibition from holding corporate or financial positions.
