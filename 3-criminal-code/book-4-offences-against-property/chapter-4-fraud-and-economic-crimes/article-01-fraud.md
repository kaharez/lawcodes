# Article 1 â€“ Fraud

1. Fraud is the act of dishonestly making a false representation, dishonestly failing to disclose information, or dishonestly abusing a position of trust, with the intention of making a gain for oneself or another, or causing loss or risk of loss to another.

2. A false representation includes:
    a. a false statement of fact, law, or intention;
    b. a representation made recklessly without knowledge of its truth or falsity;
    c. a representation implied by conduct or omission; or
    d. a representation made through digital, electronic, or automated means.

3. Fraud includes:
    a. obtaining property, services, or benefits by deception;
    b. inducing another to enter into a contract, transaction, or obligation by false representation;
    c. dishonestly failing to disclose information when under a legal duty to do so;
    d. abusing a position of trust or fiduciary responsibility for personal gain or to cause loss to another;
    e. making false claims for benefits, insurance, compensation, or entitlements;
    f. identity fraud or using another person's identity to obtain benefits or avoid obligations;
    g. investment fraud, including operating pyramid schemes, Ponzi schemes, or other fraudulent investment schemes; or
    h. digital fraud, including phishing, spoofing, or other cyber-enabled fraud.

4. Fraud is punished under Penalty Band D.

5. The penalty is aggravated to Penalty Band E where:
    a. the fraud involves substantial value or causes significant financial loss;
    b. the fraud is committed against multiple victims;
    c. the fraud is systematic, organized, or part of a criminal enterprise;
    d. the fraud exploits vulnerable persons, including elderly persons, children, or persons with disabilities;
    e. the fraud is committed by a person in a position of trust, professional responsibility, or public office;
    f. the fraud undermines public confidence in financial systems, markets, or institutions;
    g. the fraud involves sophisticated methods or exploitation of digital systems; or
    h. the offender has previous convictions for fraud or economic crimes.

6. It is not a defence that the victim was careless, gullible, or failed to take reasonable precautions.

7. Attempted fraud is punished under Penalty Band C.
