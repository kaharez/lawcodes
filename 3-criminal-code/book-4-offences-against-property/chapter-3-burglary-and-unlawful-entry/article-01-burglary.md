# Article 1 â€“ Burglary

1. Burglary is the act of entering a building, structure, or enclosed premises as a trespasser with the intention of committing theft, causing damage, or committing any other offence, or entering lawfully and remaining as a trespasser with such intention.

2. For the purposes of this Article:
    a. a person is a trespasser if they enter or remain in a place without lawful authority or the permission of the owner or lawful occupier;
    b. "building" includes any permanent or temporary structure, dwelling, commercial premises, or enclosed space; and
    c. entry includes any part of the body or any instrument controlled by the offender being introduced into the premises.

3. Burglary is punished under Penalty Band D.

4. The penalty is aggravated to Penalty Band E where:
    a. the burglary is committed in a dwelling while any person is present;
    b. the burglary involves use or threatened use of violence;
    c. the burglary involves use of a weapon or dangerous instrument;
    d. the burglary is committed by two or more persons acting in concert;
    e. the burglary causes serious damage to property or results in substantial loss;
    f. the burglary is part of a systematic scheme or organized criminal activity;
    g. the offender has previous convictions for burglary or related offences; or
    h. the burglary violates premises used for worship, education, healthcare, or other public purposes.

5. Where burglary results in actual commission of theft, assault, or other offences, those offences may be charged in addition to burglary.

6. Attempted burglary is punished under Penalty Band C.

7. Possession of tools, equipment, or instruments for the purpose of committing burglary is punished under Penalty Band C.
