# Article 3 â€“ False Accounting and Fraudulent Records

1. It is an offence to dishonestly destroy, deface, conceal, falsify, or make false entries in any accounting record, book, document, or electronic record required to be kept for accounting, regulatory, or legal purposes, with the intention of concealing facts, gaining advantage, or causing loss.

2. False accounting and fraudulent records include:
    a. falsifying financial statements, ledgers, invoices, receipts, or other accounting documents;
    b. making false entries or omissions in books of account or financial records;
    c. destroying or concealing accounting records to prevent discovery of fraud, embezzlement, or other offences;
    d. creating fictitious transactions, invoices, or entries;
    e. backdating or manipulating documents to conceal misconduct;
    f. maintaining double books or secret accounting systems;
    g. falsifying records required for tax, regulatory, or audit purposes; or
    h. using false or fraudulent documents to support claims, applications, or declarations.

3. False accounting is punished under Penalty Band D.

4. The penalty is aggravated to Penalty Band E where:
    a. the false accounting conceals substantial fraud, embezzlement, or theft;
    b. the false accounting is systematic or prolonged;
    c. the false accounting is committed by persons in professional, fiduciary, or public positions;
    d. the false accounting undermines regulatory oversight, audits, or financial supervision;
    e. the false accounting involves public funds or publicly traded companies;
    f. the false accounting facilitates money laundering, tax evasion, or other serious crimes; or
    g. the offender has previous convictions for fraud or accounting offences.

5. Juridical persons may be held liable for false accounting committed by their directors, officers, or employees in the course of business.

6. Professional accountants, auditors, and financial advisors who knowingly participate in or facilitate false accounting are subject to the penalties under this Article and may be disqualified from practicing their professions.

7. The court may order production of true accounts, restitution, and confiscation of proceeds obtained through false accounting.
