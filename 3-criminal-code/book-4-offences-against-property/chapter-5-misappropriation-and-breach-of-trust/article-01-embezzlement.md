# Article 1 â€“ Embezzlement

1. Embezzlement is the dishonest appropriation or conversion of property or funds by a person to whom the property or funds have been entrusted by virtue of employment, office, fiduciary relationship, or professional position.

2. Embezzlement includes:
    (a) appropriating money, property, or assets belonging to an employer, client, or principal;
    (b) diverting funds or property for personal use or benefit;
    (c) converting property held in trust or fiduciary capacity to one's own use;
    (d) misappropriating public funds or property by a person holding public office or employment; or
    (e) dishonestly dealing with property entrusted for safekeeping, administration, or specific purposes.

3. Embezzlement is punished under Penalty Band D.

4. The penalty is aggravated to Penalty Band E where:
    (a) the embezzled amount or value is substantial;
    (b) the embezzlement is systematic or prolonged over an extended period;
    (c) the embezzlement is committed by a person in a senior position of trust or authority;
    (d) the embezzlement involves public funds or property;
    (e) the embezzlement causes significant harm or loss to vulnerable persons, charitable organizations, or public institutions;
    (f) the embezzlement involves sophisticated schemes or falsification of records to conceal the conduct; or
    (g) the offender has previous convictions for embezzlement or breach of trust.

5. It is not a defence that the accused intended to repay or replace the embezzled property, or that the accused believed they were entitled to the property.

6. The court may order full restitution, confiscation of proceeds, and disqualification from holding positions of trust or fiduciary responsibility.
