# Article 2 â€“ Breach of Trust

1. Breach of trust is the dishonest abuse of a position of trust or fiduciary responsibility for personal gain or to cause loss to another.

2. Breach of trust includes:
    a. misusing confidential information for personal advantage or to cause harm;
    b. exploiting a position of trust to gain secret profits or benefits;
    c. acting in conflict of interest without disclosure or authorization;
    d. misusing powers, authority, or access for unauthorized purposes;
    e. breaching duties owed in a professional, fiduciary, or confidential relationship; or
    f. any other dishonest abuse of a position of trust or confidence.

3. Breach of trust is punished under Penalty Band D.

4. The penalty is aggravated to Penalty Band E where:
    a. the breach causes substantial loss or harm;
    b. the breach is committed by a person in a senior professional, fiduciary, or public position;
    c. the breach undermines public confidence in professions, institutions, or fiduciary relationships;
    d. the breach involves vulnerable victims or exploitation of dependents;
    e. the breach is systematic or part of organized criminal activity; or
    f. the offender has previous convictions for breach of trust or related offences.

5. Persons in positions of trust include, but are not limited to: trustees, guardians, curators, executors, administrators, agents, directors, officers, employees with fiduciary duties, professional advisors, and persons holding public office.

6. The court may order disgorgement of profits, restitution, and disqualification from holding positions of trust.
