# Article 2 â€“ Cyber Theft and Digital Property Crimes

1. Cyber theft is the unlawful appropriation of digital property, data, or assets through electronic or digital means, with the intention of permanently depriving the owner or causing loss.

2. Cyber theft and digital property crimes include:
    a. theft of cryptocurrencies, digital tokens, or virtual currency;
    b. theft of digital assets, including non-fungible tokens (NFTs), digital art, or other blockchain-based property;
    c. theft of data, databases, trade secrets, or confidential information;
    d. theft of computing resources, including unauthorized use of processing power or storage;
    e. ransomware attacks, including encrypting or denying access to data or systems and demanding payment for restoration;
    f. theft through phishing, social engineering, or cyber deception;
    g. SIM swapping or account takeover for the purpose of stealing digital assets or funds;
    h. manipulation or alteration of digital transactions or records to misappropriate funds or property; or
    i. any other unauthorized appropriation of property through cyber means.

3. Cyber theft is punished under Penalty Band D.

4. The penalty is aggravated to Penalty Band E where:
    a. the value of digital property stolen is substantial;
    b. the theft affects multiple victims or is part of a systematic scheme;
    c. the theft involves sophisticated technical methods or exploitation of zero-day vulnerabilities;
    d. the theft disrupts critical services, financial systems, or infrastructure;
    e. the theft is committed by organized criminal groups or networks;
    f. the theft causes substantial loss or harm to victims;
    g. the theft involves ransomware or extortion demands; or
    h. the offender has previous convictions for cybercrime.

5. Ransomware attacks are punished under Penalty Band E, regardless of whether ransom is paid or data is restored.

6. Phishing, spoofing, and social engineering for the purpose of obtaining access to accounts, credentials, or digital property are punished under Penalty Band C, or Penalty Band D where they result in actual theft or substantial attempts.

7. For the purposes of this Article:
    a. digital property includes any intangible property with economic value that exists in digital or electronic form;
    b. cryptocurrencies and digital assets are treated as property capable of being stolen;
    c. theft is complete when the offender obtains control or possession of the digital property, regardless of whether it can be converted to other forms; and
    d. the location of servers, systems, or property is immaterial where the offender or victim is subject to Kaharagian jurisdiction.

8. The court may order confiscation of stolen digital property, restoration to victims where possible, and compensation for losses.
