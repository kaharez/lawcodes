# Article 3 â€“ Computer Sabotage and Interference

1. Computer sabotage is the intentional and unauthorized interference with the functioning of computer systems, networks, data, or digital infrastructure, causing disruption, damage, or impairment.

2. Computer sabotage and interference includes:
    (a) deleting, destroying, altering, or corrupting data, programs, or systems;
    (b) introducing malware, viruses, worms, trojans, or other malicious code;
    (c) conducting denial-of-service or distributed denial-of-service attacks;
    (d) disabling, disrupting, or degrading the performance of computer systems or networks;
    (e) interfering with the integrity, availability, or functionality of data or systems;
    (f) manipulating, falsifying, or corrupting data or records;
    (g) sabotaging backups, security measures, or recovery systems; or
    (h) any other act causing harm, disruption, or impairment to computer systems or data.

3. Computer sabotage and interference against private or commercial systems is punished under Penalty Band D.

4. The penalty is aggravated to Penalty Band E where:
    (a) the sabotage or interference affects critical infrastructure, essential services, or systems serving the public;
    (b) the sabotage or interference causes extensive damage, disruption, or economic loss;
    (c) the sabotage or interference endangers life, health, or safety;
    (d) the sabotage or interference affects multiple systems or victims;
    (e) the sabotage or interference is committed by organized groups or for terrorist purposes;
    (f) the sabotage or interference facilitates other serious crimes; or
    (g) the offender has previous convictions for cybercrime.

5. Computer sabotage and interference against State systems is subject to more severe penalties under Book 2, Chapter III, Article 4.

6. Creation, distribution, or provision of malicious software, tools, or services for the purpose of sabotage or interference is punished under Penalty Band C, or Penalty Band D where the tools are specifically designed for serious sabotage or are distributed on a commercial scale.

7. It is not a defence that:
    (a) the systems had inadequate security measures;
    (b) the sabotage or interference was committed remotely or from outside any territorial jurisdiction;
    (c) the damage or disruption was temporary or could be remedied; or
    (d) the offender claimed to be conducting security research or testing.

8. The court may order restitution, compensation for losses, and prohibition from accessing or working with computer systems.

9. This Article applies to sabotage and interference committed anywhere in the world by nationals or Verified Kaharagians, or affecting systems or data in which Kaharagian persons or entities have rights or interests.
