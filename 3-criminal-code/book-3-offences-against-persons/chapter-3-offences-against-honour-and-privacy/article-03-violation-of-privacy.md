# Article 3 – Violation of Privacy

1. Any person who unlawfully intrudes upon the private life, communications, personal data, or digital spaces of another person commits an offence.

2. Violation of privacy includes:
    (a) accessing personal or private communications without consent;
    (b) obtaining personal data through deception, hacking, or unauthorised means;
    (c) recording or distributing private information, images, or correspondence without lawful authority;
    (d) tracking or monitoring another person’s digital activity without consent.

3. This offence is punishable within Penalty Band C, or Penalty Band D where the intrusion is extensive, harmful, or committed for blackmail, exploitation, or intimidation.
