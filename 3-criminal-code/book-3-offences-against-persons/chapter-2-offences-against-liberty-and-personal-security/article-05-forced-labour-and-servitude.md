# Article 5 â€“ Forced Labour and Servitude

1. Forced labour is work or service extracted from a person under threat of penalty, coercion, or abuse of vulnerability, and for which the person has not offered themselves voluntarily.

2. Servitude is a condition of dependency in which a person is obliged to live and work for another person, under coercion or abuse of authority, and from which the person cannot escape.

3. The following acts constitute offences:
    (a) requiring, compelling, or coercing a person to perform forced labour or servitude (Penalty Band E);
    (b) recruiting, transporting, transferring, harbouring, or receiving persons for the purpose of forced labour or servitude (Penalty Band E);
    (c) exploiting forced labour or servitude, including by profiting from or benefiting from the work or service of a person in forced labour or servitude (Penalty Band D);
    (d) facilitating, enabling, or aiding forced labour or servitude (Penalty Band D);
    (e) confiscating, withholding, or destroying identity documents, travel documents, or work permits to compel labour or servitude (Penalty Band D); or
    (f) threatening, intimidating, or punishing a person to compel labour or prevent escape from servitude (Penalty Band D).

4. For the purposes of this Article, forced labour and servitude include:
    (a) forcing a person to work without pay or with inadequate compensation;
    (b) subjecting a person to excessive or degrading working conditions;
    (c) restricting a person's freedom of movement to compel work;
    (d) threatening a person with violence, deportation, or harm to family members to compel work;
    (e) exploiting debt bondage or financial dependency to compel work;
    (f) exploiting a person's immigration status, lack of legal rights, or vulnerability to compel work; or
    (g) subjecting a person to conditions of slavery, slavery-like practices, or practices similar to slavery.

5. It is not a defence that:
    (a) the victim agreed to the work or entered into a contract;
    (b) the victim received some payment or benefit;
    (c) the victim did not attempt to escape or seek help;
    (d) the work was performed in another jurisdiction;
    (e) the conditions of labour or servitude are customary or accepted in a particular industry or region; or
    (f) the victim was indebted to the offender.

6. The penalty is aggravated where:
    (a) the victim is a child under the age of eighteen years;
    (b) the forced labour or servitude is prolonged or systematic;
    (c) the forced labour or servitude involves multiple victims;
    (d) the forced labour or servitude results in serious physical or psychological harm;
    (e) the forced labour or servitude involves particularly dangerous, degrading, or harmful work;
    (f) the offender is part of an organized criminal network or enterprise; or
    (g) the forced labour or servitude is accompanied by violence, confinement, or severe abuse.

7. Where forced labour or servitude is combined with sexual exploitation, the provisions of Book 3, Chapter 6, Article 6 also apply.

8. The court may order restitution of wages, compensation for harm suffered, and protective measures for the victim.
