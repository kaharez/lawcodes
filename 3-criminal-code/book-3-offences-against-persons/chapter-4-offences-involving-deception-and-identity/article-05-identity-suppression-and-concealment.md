# Article 5 â€“ Identity Suppression and Concealment

1. Any person who intentionally suppresses, conceals, or misrepresents their identity for the purpose of committing or facilitating an offence commits an offence.

2. Identity suppression includes:
    (a) use of false identities or anonymous communication to evade responsibility;
    (b) altering or erasing identifying information to conceal involvement in wrongdoing;
    (c) digitally masking identity to commit offences or deceive authorities.

3. This offence is punishable within Penalty Band B, or Penalty Band C where it is linked to another offence or used to mislead State or judicial authorities.
