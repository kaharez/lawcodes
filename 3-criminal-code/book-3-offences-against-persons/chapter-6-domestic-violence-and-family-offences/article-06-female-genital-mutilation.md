# Article 6 â€“ Female Genital Mutilation

1. Female genital mutilation is any procedure that involves the partial or total removal, alteration, or injury of the external female genitalia for non-medical reasons.

2. Female genital mutilation includes, but is not limited to:
    a. clitoridectomy (removal of the clitoris);
    b. excision (removal of the clitoris and labia minora);
    c. infibulation (narrowing of the vaginal opening);
    d. pricking, piercing, incising, scraping, or cauterizing the genital area for non-medical purposes; or
    e. any other procedure that damages or alters the female genitalia for cultural, religious, or traditional reasons.

3. The following acts constitute offences under this Article:
    a. performing or carrying out female genital mutilation (Penalty Band E);
    b. assisting, aiding, or facilitating female genital mutilation (Penalty Band E);
    c. arranging, procuring, or causing female genital mutilation to be performed (Penalty Band E);
    d. taking or causing a person to be taken to another jurisdiction for the purpose of female genital mutilation (Penalty Band E);
    e. failing to protect a girl or woman under one's care from female genital mutilation, where the person has authority or responsibility and knows or ought to know of the risk (Penalty Band D); or
    f. attempting or conspiring to commit any of the foregoing acts (Penalty Band D).

4. Female genital mutilation is an offence regardless of:
    a. the age of the victim;
    b. whether the victim or the victim's family consented to the procedure;
    c. whether the procedure was performed for cultural, religious, traditional, or social reasons;
    d. whether the procedure was performed by a medical professional or traditional practitioner;
    e. the jurisdiction in which the procedure occurred or was intended to occur; or
    f. whether physical harm or complications resulted from the procedure.

5. It is not a defence that:
    a. the procedure is customary or traditional in the victim's family or community;
    b. the victim requested or consented to the procedure;
    c. the victim's parents or guardians consented to the procedure;
    d. the procedure was performed in the context of a ceremony or rite of passage; or
    e. the procedure is lawful in another jurisdiction.

6. The penalty is aggravated where:
    a. the victim is a child under the age of fourteen years;
    b. the procedure results in serious physical injury, complications, or death;
    c. the procedure was performed without medical safeguards or in unsafe conditions;
    d. multiple victims are subjected to the procedure;
    e. the offender is a person in a position of trust, authority, or familial relationship with the victim; or
    f. the offence is part of an organized or systematic practice involving multiple perpetrators.

7. It is a defence if the procedure was performed by a medical professional for genuine medical purposes, including treatment of disease, injury, or abnormality, or for legitimate health reasons.

8. The court may recommend to The Sovereign that loss of nationality be considered in cases involving grave or systematic commission of female genital mutilation.

9. The State shall take all necessary measures to prevent, protect against, and educate the population about the harm and illegality of female genital mutilation.
