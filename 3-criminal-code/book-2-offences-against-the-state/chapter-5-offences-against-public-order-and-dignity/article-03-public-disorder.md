# Article 3 â€“ Public Disorder

1. Any person who engages in behaviour that disrupts public decency, order, or the dignity of the State commits an offence.

2. Public disorder includes:
    (a) conduct causing serious disturbance to lawful assembly, ceremony, or religious observance;
    (b) obscene or degrading acts committed in a public or shared digital space;
    (c) behaviour intended to provoke alarm or offend public morals.

3. This offence is punishable within Penalty Band A, or Penalty Band B where the conduct is intentionally provocative or repeated.
