# Article 4 – Sabotage of Digital Infrastructure

1. Any person who intentionally damages, disrupts, disables, or impairs the functioning of State digital infrastructure commits an offence.

2. This includes acts directed toward:
    a. identity and credential systems;
    b. registry services or public portals;
    c. authentication servers, code-signing systems, or cryptographic keys;
    d. digital platforms used for justice, administration, or official publication.

3. Sabotage of digital infrastructure is punishable within Penalty Band D, or Penalty Band E where it:
    a. endangers national security;
    b. corrupts legal or judicial records;
    c. affects nationality, honours, or identity systems;
    d. threatens the continuity of the State’s digital governance.
